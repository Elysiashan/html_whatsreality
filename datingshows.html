Dating Shows

Love Island USA

From Peacock

About:Love Island is a dating show where a group of single “islanders” are sent to the “villa” to get to know each other and form connections. These islanders are excluded from the outside world and can only interact with others in the Villa. After a few days the villa has a recoupling where they must pair up with each other and the recoupling is where they must couple up with the same connection or someone else depending on the connections. They have a few fun challenges every few episodes that cause more drama and messiness between the contestants.

Show host: Ariana Madix | Best seasons: 2, 5, 6

Love Island has 7 seasons and are typically aired during the summer. In addition to Love Island USA, there is also the same show but in different locations like Love Island UK and Australia. The episodes are a hour long and are played every day during the summer. You can typically watch ove Island USA on Peacock.

Bachelor/Bachelorette

From Andy Denhart

About:A single male or female dates many people of the opposite sex throughout the season to see who they’re most compatible with by going on one-on-one dates and having deep conversations. Each week one contestant is eliminated and by the end of the season they offer a rose to the last contestant.

Show host: Jesse Palmer | Best seasons for Bachelor: 17, 13, 28 | Best seasons for Bachelorette: 1, 11, 5

90 Day Fiancé

From Us Weekly

 About:This dating show follows American citizens and their foreign fiancés through the K-1 visa journey. The show starts with them traveling to America and starting the 90 days for them to get married until the visa expires and their journey of living together. Each couple faces many different roadblocks, being unable to work, travel, family drama, or feeling lonely in a new country.

Show host: Shaun Robinson/Sukanya Krishnan | Best seasons: 1, 2, 4

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by 90 Day Fiance (@90dayfiance)

90 Day Fiancé has 11 seasons, and the seasons typically end with the final episode or an extra episode as the tell all episode where the audience is reunited with the couples and they get asked burning questions and the stance of where their relationship is. In addition to this show, they also have many spin off series like 90 Day Fiancé: Happily Ever After which documents couples after their 90 days, and we get to see their lives happily married. You can watch many of these shows on Hulu and Disney+.
